## 📅 Course Timeline & Curriculum

### **Week 1: Groundwork and Gear-Up**
Before we build Skynet, we need to know how to use a wrench. This week is all about laying a rock-solid foundation.
* **The Big Picture (Theory):** What actually *is* ML? We will cover the ML Life Cycle (data, training, evaluation, inference), the difference between supervised and unsupervised learning, and the holy grail of optimization: Gradient Descent. 
* **The Toolbelt (Practical):** We will get our hands dirty with the absolute prerequisites. You will learn how to wrangle data using Python, Pandas, and NumPy, visualize it with Matplotlib, and run it all seamlessly in Jupyter/Colab notebooks. 
* **Data Pre-processing:** Real-world data is messy. You will learn how to clean basic text, create train/val/test splits, and we'll touch briefly on Tokenization so you understand how machines read words.

### **Week 2: Respecting the Classics & Entering the Deep End**
We will take a quick look at how things *used* to be done before diving headfirst into modern Deep Learning. 
* **Classical ML:** A brief overview of Bag-of-Words, TF-IDF, and Logistic Regression for sentiment analysis. We will focus mostly on *why* these have limitations compared to modern tech.
* **Evaluation Metrics:** How do you know if your model is actually smart or just guessing? We will decode loss, accuracy, F1 scores, recall, and the ever-dreaded Confusion Matrix.
* **Into the Neural Network:** Meet the perceptron. We will build up to Multi-Layer Perceptrons (MLPs), explore hidden layers, and understand activation functions (ReLU, tanh, softmax).
* **Embeddings:** How do we turn words into math? We will look at the flaws of one-hot encoding and introduce proper vector embeddings like Word2Vec.
* **🛠️ Mini-Project:** You will build a small text analyzer or name completer to put the theory to the test.

### **Week 3: Transformers & The AI Revolution**
*Attention is All You Need* (literally, please pay attention this week). We are getting into the architecture that powers today's AI.
* **The Transformer Architecture:** A demystified, high-level overview of how Transformers actually work.
* **Encoder vs. Decoder:** Understanding the difference between models that understand text (like BERT) and models that generate text (like ChatGPT).
* **HuggingFace API:** Why train from scratch when you can borrow? We will use HuggingFace pipelines for sentiment analysis, translation, and building chatbots.
* **Modern LLM Paradigms:** Theory on how the massive models are trained and controlled: Few-shot prompting, Chain-of-Thought (CoT), RLHF, and SFT.
* **Introduction to RAG:** A theoretical intro to Retrieval-Augmented Generation and Vector Databases—giving your AI a memory.
* **🛠️ Mini-Project:** Building a simple, functional console-based chatbot.

### **Week 4: The Final Project & The Bleeding Edge**
You survived the first three weeks. Now, we build something cool and take a sneak peek at the future.
* **🛠️ The Final Project:** Building an **IITB AI Assistant**. You will prompt a major cloud model, integrate RAG so it actually knows about the insti, and wrap it all in a clean, interactive web app using Streamlit or Gradio. Finally, an assistant that can answer your queries without complaining!
* **Beyond the Horizon:** We will wrap up the course by introducing the other massive domains in ML:
    * Reinforcement Learning (how AI learns to play games)
    * Generative Vision & Audio (Stable Diffusion)
    * Agentic AI and Tool Calling (AI that actually *does* things for you)
    * The Bleeding Edge (SSMs, JEPA, and what is coming next)
